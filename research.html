01 · Research goal
How can an interactive learning experience help users recognize the connection between their short-video behavior, algorithmic recommendations, and consumer influence?

I wanted to understand how college students experience short-video recommendation systems and whether they notice how their own actions shape the feed over time.

This project began as a course-based concept. After the semester ended, I independently rebuilt the project website and prototype, using the original research direction as the foundation.

03 · Key findings

Key Findings

The research pointed to three gaps that shaped the prototype.

01
Finding

Users know algorithms exist, but not how they train the feed.

Supporting evidence

Interviews and expert feedback pointed to a gap between general awareness and critical understanding.

Design implication

Let users interact first, then reveal the behavior-to-feed connection.

02
Finding

Repeated exposure makes consumer content feel natural.

Supporting evidence

Product and creator content often appears as lifestyle, trend, or recommendation content rather than as a clear sales pitch.

Design implication

Show influence as gradual category shifts, not a single obvious ad.

03
Finding

Personalization can feel like preference.

Supporting evidence

Students described feeds as personal, even when repeated exposure may have shaped what felt appealing.

Design implication

Compare stated intention with observed behavior after the session.

04 · Feedback loop

How the feedback loop becomes invisible.

The research logic is a continuous sequence, not a single moment of influence.

01A user pauses or likes
02The system reads it as a signal
03Similar content appears more often
04Repeated exposure feels familiar
05Familiarity begins to feel like preference

The influence does not happen through a single post. It accumulates through repeated interactions and repeated exposure.

05 · Design implications

From Research to Design

Each finding was translated into a prototype decision.

→

Research insight Repeated exposure feels natural rather than persuasive.
→

→

06 · Audience

Target Learner

A learner profile synthesized from interview patterns and the project context.

Learner persona

Emma, 20

College student / Daily short-video user

A college student who uses short-video platforms daily and occasionally purchases products discovered through creator content.

She understands that recommendation systems personalize her feed, but rarely connects individual interactions with how that personalization develops.

Pain Points

  • Knows the feed is personalized, but not how individual actions shape it.
  • Experiences repeated product exposure as natural discovery.
  • Rarely reflects on the difference between intention and behavior.

Learner Needs

  • A concrete way to observe the feedback loop.
  • An experience that feels familiar rather than instructional.
  • A reflection moment connecting behavior with recommendation outcomes.
Research direction

Make the feedback loop visible.

The research led to a simulation built around action, pattern recognition, and reflection.

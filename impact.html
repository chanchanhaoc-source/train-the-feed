02 · Questions

Evaluation Goals

Does the experience help learners understand how short-video actions shape recommendations and consumer decisions?

Goal 01

Algorithm Awareness

Can learners explain how likes, skips, saves, and watch time shape future recommendations?

Goal 02

Persuasive Content Recognition

Can learners identify influence in lifestyle, trend, influencer, and product content?

Goal 03

Reflection

Can learners connect the simulation to their own scrolling and purchasing habits?

Goal 04

Usability

Can learners complete the experience without confusion or friction?

03 · Evidence

Success Metrics

Success is defined through observable understanding, recognition, reflection, usability, and transfer.

01 Algorithm Awareness

Describe the action → recommendation → repetition loop.

02 Persuasive Recognition

Identify at least two persuasive strategies in short-video content.

03 Reflection

Connect the simulation to personal media habits.

04 Usability

Complete the prototype with minimal confusion.

05 Transfer

Create one realistic strategy for intentional digital consumption.

04 · Planned evaluation

Test with the intended learners.

The next step is a focused study with college students who regularly use short-video platforms.

Testing Setup

  • 5–8 college students
  • Think-aloud usability testing
  • Short post-experience interview
  • Pre/post reflection questions
  • Observe confusion and drop-off points

What I Would Look For

  • Whether learners understand why the feed changed
  • Whether the pattern reveal feels meaningful
  • Whether persuasion is recognized without direct instruction
  • Whether prompts feel personal but not judgmental
  • Whether the final strategy feels realistic
05 · Future refinement

Iteration Priorities

Future iterations would focus on clarity, realism, reflection, and accessibility.

01

Clearer Recommendation Shifts

Strengthen feedback or the summary if learners do not notice how the feed changes.

02

More Content Variety

Add categories and content patterns so the feed feels less predictable.

03

Better Reflection Prompts

Connect prompts to late-night scrolling, product discovery, and impulse purchases.

04

Accessibility Improvements

Continue reviewing contrast, text size, keyboard navigation, and reduced motion.

06 · Personal reflection

This project brought learning experience design and interaction design together.

The key shift was realizing that algorithm awareness is not only an information problem. Many users already know algorithms exist. The harder challenge is helping them notice how small actions shape the feed over time.

Building the prototype pushed me to translate learning goals into actions, feedback moments, prompts, and interface states. Codex and Claude Code helped with implementation, but the core design work was the learning sequence, interaction logic, and reflection structure.

02 · Proposed measures

Evaluation Criteria

These proposed measures are ready for formative testing. They are not completed impact results.

Dimension 01

Algorithm Awareness

Evaluation question

Can learners explain how their interactions shaped the feed?

Proposed evidence

Post-experience explanation of the connection between repeated interactions and recommendation changes.

Dimension 02

Persuasive Content Recognition

Evaluation question

Can learners identify commercial influence in creator-style content?

Proposed evidence

A post-experience content-recognition prompt.

Dimension 03

Reflection

Evaluation question

Can learners compare their stated preference with their observed behavior?

Proposed evidence

A written or verbal reflection response after the reveal.

Dimension 04

Usability

Evaluation question

Can learners complete the experience without unnecessary confusion?

Proposed evidence

Completion behavior, observed errors, navigation issues, and participant feedback.

03 · Future testing

Proposed Testing Plan

A focused plan for testing whether the prototype supports awareness, reflection, and usable interaction.

Participants10 college students, ages 18–25
FormatModerated usability and learning-response sessions
Estimated duration20–30 minutes per participant

Activities

  • Complete the prototype
  • Think aloud during the experience
  • Respond to the reveal
  • Answer post-experience reflection questions
  • Participate in a short follow-up interview

Testing sequence

Pre-question → Experience → Reveal → Reflection → Interview
04 · Future refinement

Iteration Priorities

Future iterations should focus on the parts most likely to affect understanding and completion.

01

Clearer Recommendation Shifts

Make category changes recognizable without exposing hidden scores during the feed.

02

More Content Variety

Add more examples within each category to reduce repetition.

03

Better Reflection Prompts

Help learners connect specific interactions with the final feed outcome.

04

Accessibility Improvements

Improve keyboard navigation, focus states, readable contrast, and reduced-motion support.

05 · Reflection

What the prototype makes possible.

What I learned

The project showed how learning experience design can become interactive: algorithm awareness became a sequence of intention, behavior, feedback, and reflection rather than a written explanation.

What I would improve

Before claiming learning impact, the next version needs user testing to see whether learners notice the feed shift, understand the reveal, and connect the simplified simulation to real scrolling habits.

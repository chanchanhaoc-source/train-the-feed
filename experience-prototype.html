How the experience works

The interface keeps the scoring logic hidden during the feed, then uses the final reveal to turn behavior into evidence. The simulation simplifies recommendation logic to make the feedback loop observable; it is not intended to model a real platform algorithm.

03 · Reflect

The report shows what happened. Reflection turns it into something useful.

Use your own likes and cart as evidence while answering these prompts.

Question 01

Look at your cart. Which item surprised you the most—and do you actually want it, or does it just feel familiar now?

Think about the difference between genuinely wanting something and having been shown it enough times that it started to feel like your idea.

Question 02

At what point did you notice the feed shifting? Or did you not notice at all—and what does that tell you?

If you did not notice the shift, that is useful evidence too: the feed is designed to feel natural.

Question 03

If you opened your real For You page right now, what would you look for differently?

Be concrete: a word, a pattern, or a feeling. Vague awareness does not change behavior.
